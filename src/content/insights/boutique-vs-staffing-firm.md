When a startup starts talking to agencies, the first fork is size: the big staffing brand with offices everywhere, or the boutique that only does your niche. Founders often assume bigger means safer. In technical search, the relationship between size and quality is nothing like that simple.

## What scale is genuinely good at

Large staffing firms excel at volume and breadth: many roles, many locations, contract staffing, compliance, speed to warm bodies. If you need fifteen QA contractors across three cities by next month, scale is exactly the right tool and boutiques cannot compete.

## Where scale works against you

Search quality lives in the individual recruiter, and big-firm economics push their best people toward the biggest accounts. A startup's two searches land with whoever has capacity, often a junior recruiter running twenty roles in parallel, keyword-matching across a giant but shallow database. The brand on the invoice is not the person doing your work. Ask who, specifically, will run your search and how many other roles they carry; the answer is usually clarifying.

## What a boutique actually sells

A specialist boutique sells depth in a narrow lane: a mapped candidate network in your exact niche, pattern recognition across many similar searches, and senior people doing the actual work because there is no junior bench to hide one. The trade-off is capacity and breadth; a good boutique says no when the role is outside its lane. Treat that honesty as a feature.

## The screening difference

Depth also shows in assessment. Volume businesses screen for keyword match and availability; search practices screen for fit. We run psychometric and culture screening on every shortlist, which is not economical at staffing-firm volume and is exactly the point.

## Match the tool to the job

Contract volume and commodity roles: big firm, no shame in it. Senior, scarce, team-defining technical hires: the specialist wins on the only metrics that matter, shortlist quality and retention. If your roles are the second kind, book a demo and judge the depth yourself.
